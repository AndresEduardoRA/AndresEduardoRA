## Working on Real Problems

Around this time, I'm starting to take on small independent projects mostly through referrals from friends and family.

These aren't large commercial projects, but they're real:

- Small business websites
- Simple management systems
- Tools to automate specific tasks

The key difference from practice projects: real users with real needs.

## What This Is Teaching Me

**Communication is crucial**: Understanding what clients actually need (vs. what they say they want) is a skill.

**Requirements change**: What seems clear at the start often evolves as we work together.

**Delivery matters**: It's not done until it's deployed and the client can use it.
AI helps me summarize requirements and prepare simple agreements so expectations stay aligned.

**Support is part of the job**: After delivery, there are questions, small fixes, and adjustments.

## Balancing Learning and Work

I'm still a full-time student, so I have to balance:

- University coursework
- Intensive self-study
- These small projects
- Personal time (though I'm sacrificing a lot of this)

Time management is becoming essential.

## Growth Focus

This experience is helping me develop:

- Professional communication skills
- Understanding business requirements
- Delivering complete, working solutions
- Managing expectations and timelines
