## The Experience

In February 2025, I'm participating in the Camballey Hackathon another opportunity to build something quickly with a team.

After my NASA Space Apps experience, I feel more prepared for the hackathon format, but it's still challenging.

## What We're Building

Our team is developing a solution to improve public transportation in my hometown, Santa Cruz de la Sierra, Bolivia. Many commuters struggle with unclear routes, limited real-time information, and inefficient connections between transport options.

The process involves:

- Brainstorming and validating ideas quickly
- Dividing responsibilities among team members
- Building a working prototype
- Preparing a presentation for judges
 
We use AI to draft requirement maps and outline the pitch, keeping communication crisp while we iterate fast.

## What I'm Learning

**Execution beats perfect ideas**: Many teams have interesting ideas, but the ones that ship working prototypes stand out.

**Time constraints force prioritization**: We have to focus on core features and cut everything else.

**Team dynamics matter**: Clear communication and defined roles help us move faster.

**MVP thinking is essential**: Building the minimum viable product first, then adding features if time allows.

## Improvements from Last Time

- Better time management
- More realistic scope planning
- Clearer team communication
- Faster decision-making

## Challenges

- Balancing feature completeness with time constraints
- Integrating different parts of the solution
- Staying focused under pressure
- Managing energy over the event duration

## Growth

This experience is reinforcing:

- Rapid prototyping skills
- Team collaboration abilities
- Product thinking and prioritization
- Presentation skills
