## The Experience

In October 2024, I'm participating in the NASA Space Apps Challenge a global hackathon where teams build solutions using NASA's open data.

This is my first major hackathon experience, and it's teaching me a lot about:

- Working with a team under time pressure
- Building something functional in 48 hours
- Presenting technical ideas to judges
- Collaborating with people I just met

## What We're Building

Our team is building a prototype that [uses NASA data to solve a specific challenge]. We have to:

- Quickly understand the problem
- Divide work among team members
- Integrate different parts of the solution
- Present our work effectively
 
We also use AI assistants to brainstorm directions and draft technical notes, so communication stays clear while we move fast.

## What I'm Learning

**Speed matters in hackathons**: We have to make quick decisions and move fast. Perfect code isn't the goal working code is.

**Collaboration is key**: Working with teammates I just met requires clear communication and flexibility.

**Scope management is crucial**: We have to cut features to finish on time. Prioritization is essential.

**Presentation matters**: Building something great doesn't matter if you can't explain it clearly.

## Challenges

- Time pressure is intense
- Integrating work from multiple people is tricky
- Some technical challenges are taking longer than expected
- Balancing sleep, food, and coding is hard

## Growth

This experience is helping me develop:

- Rapid prototyping skills
- Team collaboration under pressure
- Ability to work with unfamiliar teammates
- Presentation and communication skills
